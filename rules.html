Rules of Chess

There are special moves in chess:

Castling: A move involving the king and one of
the rooks, where the king moves two squares
towards a rook and the rook moves to the square
next to the king.
En passant: A pawn capture that can occur when a
pawn moves two squares forward from its starting
position, allowing an opposing pawn to capture
it as if it had moved only one square forward.
Pawn promotion: When a pawn reaches the opposite
side of the board, it can be promoted to any
other piece (except a king) of the same color.

Players must also be mindful of the rules of check and checkmate:

Check: When a player's king is under direct threat
of capture by the opponent's pieces.
Checkmate: When a player's king is in check and there
is no legal move to escape the threat, resulting in
the end of the game.
